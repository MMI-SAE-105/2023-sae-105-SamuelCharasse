New North Zealand Hospital - Hillerød, Denmark

Urban Integration Beyond Boundaries

The FORUM UZH is more than a building; it is a testament to the harmonious integration of education and urbanity. The terraces and central plaza serve as connective threads, weaving the university into the fabric of the city. This bold approach reinforces the idea that the pursuit of knowledge should not be confined within campus walls but should extend its reach into the public domain, fostering a sense of shared learning and collaboration.

A Crown Atop the Old Town

Perched majestically above the old town of Zurich, the university quarter, with the FORUM UZH as its jewel, proudly "crowns" the landscape. This architectural feat pays homage to the 19th-century ideals that elevated knowledge and culture to paramount importance. As a modern interpretation of historicizing and classicist structures, the FORUM UZH aligns itself with the grandeur of Rämistrasse, where old city walls once stood, creating a visual symphony that resonates with both the past and the present.

Open Doors to Education and Research

What sets the FORUM UZH apart is its openness to the public. Beyond being an educational and research center, it serves as a welcoming space where the city and the university seamlessly converge. Herzog & De Meuron's design transforms the traditional perception of campus boundaries, encouraging a dynamic exchange between scholars and the public. The FORUM UZH becomes a shared space, where ideas flow freely, transcending the confines of academia.

Historical Echoes along Rämistrasse

Winding along Rämistrasse, the FORUM UZH becomes a living chapter in Zurich's rich history. Lined with historic buildings, this thoroughfare tells a story of continuity, where the pursuit of knowledge has been a constant thread. Herzog & De Meuron's design aligns with this historical context, creating a dialogue between the old and the new—a conversation that echoes the city's commitment to intellectual and cultural advancement.
