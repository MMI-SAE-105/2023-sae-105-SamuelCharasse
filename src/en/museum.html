Grand Canal Museum Complex

Grand Canal Museum Complex - Hangzhou, China

Connecting Past and Present

As the former imperial capital at the southern end of the Grand Canal, Hangzhou holds a unique place in China's narrative. Herzog & De Meuron's proposal seeks to seamlessly weave the past and present, acknowledging the canal's role in shaping China's development. The museum complex serves as a testament to the enduring legacy of the Grand Canal and its profound impact on urbanization along its historic banks.

Strategic Location

Situated at the pivotal junction of the Grand Canal and the Hanggang River, the museum complex is enveloped by water on three sides. This strategic positioning not only emphasizes the significance of the Grand Canal but also connects the complex to other large urban development areas in Hangzhou's north. The design capitalizes on this unique location to create a contemporary landmark that resonates with the city's cultural and architectural identity.

Reflecting Cultural and Natural Landscapes

Herzog & De Meuron's vision for the Grand Canal Museum Complex is rooted in reflecting the essence of Chinese cultural and natural landscapes. The design embodies a harmonious coexistence between the man-made and the natural, paying homage to the canal's vital role in shaping the nation's cultural tapestry. The museum complex is poised to become a dynamic space where history, culture, and modernity converge.

Vibrant Contemporary Gathering Place

Beyond its role as a repository of history, the Grand Canal Museum Complex aspires to be a vibrant, contemporary gathering place. Herzog & De Meuron envisions the space as a hub for cultural exchange, community engagement, and artistic expression. The design, with its thoughtful integration of indoor and outdoor spaces, aims to create an inviting environment for locals and visitors alike.
