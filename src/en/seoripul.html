Seoripul Open Art Storage

Seoripul Open Art Storage - Seoul, South Korea

A fusion of preservation and innovation

Nestled on an untouched site at the Eastern Border of Seoripul Park, the Seoripul Open Art Storage seamlessly integrates preservation with innovation. The design is a testament to Herzog & De Meuron's commitment to pushing the boundaries of conventional architecture, offering a visually stunning and functional space for the preservation of Seoul's cultural treasures.

Beyond storage

More than just a repository for invaluable artworks, the Seoripul Open Art Storage aspires to be a dynamic civic space. Herzog & De Meuron's vision extends beyond the conventional role of a storage facility, aiming to create an environment that engages Seoul residents and global visitors alike. The incorporation of interactive exhibits, public spaces, and community events will transform this archive into a vibrant cultural hub.

Strategic location

Situated adjacent to the Seoripul Performance Art Center, the Seoripul Open Art Storage strategically enhances Seocho-Dong’s identity. This location, at the Eastern Border of Seoripul Park, adds a new dimension to the district, extending its cultural significance beyond its existing role as a judicial and educational hub on Seoul’s cultural map.

A landmark for Seoul's cultural legacy

As Herzog & De Meuron redefine the concept of art storage, the Seoripul Open Art Storage is poised to become a landmark representing Seoul's rich cultural legacy. The project not only emphasizes the preservation of artistic treasures but also seeks to create an inclusive space that fosters cultural exchange and community engagement.
