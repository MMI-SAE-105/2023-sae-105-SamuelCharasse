Currie Park Towers

Currie Park Towers - West Palm Beach, Florida, USA

A Tapestry of Towers and Greenery

The heart of the project is embodied in two gracefully designed towers seamlessly embedded within a park-like landscape. Stretching from the verdant expanse of Currie Park to the neighboring residential district, the design prioritizes the integration of nature into urban living. One hundred and fifty-two apartments, adorned with extensive shaded terraces, harmoniously share space within a low plinth, ensuring minimal disruption to the surrounding streets and neighborhood.

Tropical Elegance Over Elevated Platforms

In a departure from the conventional elevated podium, Herzog & de Meuron's design for Currie Park places emphasis on tropical planting and unobstructed views. The architectural vision is clear – to prioritize the resident's connection with nature, fostering an environment where the lush green surroundings become an integral part of daily life.

Reflecting Cultural and Natural Landscapes

The Currie Park project stands as a testament to Herzog & de Meuron's commitment to sustainability and resilience. Uniquely attuned to the specific conditions of South Florida's climate, the design incorporates extensive shaded terraces, provisions for photovoltaic cells in the facade, and innovative rainwater collection features. These elements not only enhance the residents' quality of life but also showcase a forward-thinking approach to environmental responsibility.

Pedestrian-Friendly Design and Community Integration

At street level, the project unfolds as an extensive park-like landscape, elevating the pedestrian experience and seamlessly connecting residents to the vibrant city life. An activated, public street-level program reinforces the project's ties to the surrounding city, fostering a sense of community and engagement.
