New Bordeaux Stadium

New Bordeaux Stadium - Bordeaux, France

A Symphony of Light and Openness

The Bordeaux stadium stands as a beacon of light and openness. Its elegant silhouette, a departure from the bulky profiles often associated with large stadiums, evokes a sense of refinement. Herzog & De Meuron have achieved an architectural feat where simplicity meets sophistication, and the result is a structure that captivates with its aesthetic purity.

Geometrical Clarity and Monumentality

The stadium's geometrical clarity serves as the foundation for its monumental presence. Unlike traditional sporting arenas, where size often overshadows elegance, Herzog & De Meuron's design introduces a new paradigm. The Bordeaux stadium challenges preconceived notions, proving that a building of this scale can indeed embody both monumentality and gracefulness.

A Contemporary Temple of Sport

Drawing inspiration from classical architecture, the Bordeaux stadium resonates with the purity and grandeur associated with classical temples. However, it transcends this comparison by redefining the relationship between inside and outside. Unlike the elevated plinth of a traditional temple, the grand stairs of the stadium blur these boundaries, inviting spectators into a space that seamlessly merges with its surroundings.

Columns as Sentinels of Accessibility

One cannot overlook the countless columns that stand as sentinels on the stadium's grand stairs. Accompanying visitors on their journey in and out of the arena, these columns form a powerful gesture of openness and accessibility. Herzog & De Meuron have ingeniously fused the elements of stairs and columns, creating an architectural language that not only guides but also welcomes all who approach the stadium.
