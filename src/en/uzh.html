Forum UZH - Zurich, Switzerland

A Union of Urbanity and Academia

The FORUM UZH stands as a testament to Herzog & De Meuron's ability to weave academic spaces into the fabric of urban life. The terraces and central plaza serve not only as architectural elements but as bridges that connect the university to the city. In this symbiotic relationship, the city extends its embrace into the academic realm, fostering a unique integration of knowledge and urban elegance.

A Crown for Knowledge

Perched atop the old town of Zurich, the university quarter impressively "crowns" the landscape, echoing the historical significance attached to knowledge and culture in the 19th century. Herzog & De Meuron pays homage to this rich heritage by aligning the new FORUM UZH with historicizing, classicist public buildings along Rämistrasse. Here, where the old city walls once stood, a modern architectural marvel now stands, harmonizing the past and the present.

An Open Door to Education

What makes the FORUM UZH truly remarkable is its openness to the public. Breaking down traditional barriers, the new education and research center extends an invitation for everyone to step into the world of academia. Herzog & De Meuron's design transforms the university into a shared space, blurring the lines between the campus and the city, and encouraging a vibrant exchange of ideas and experiences.

Historical Echoes Along Rämistrasse

As the FORUM UZH takes shape, it not only symbolizes progress but also pays homage to the historical context along Rämistrasse. Lined with classicist public buildings, this thoroughfare becomes a living testament to the evolution of Zurich, where the past gracefully meets the future, and where the pursuit of knowledge has been a constant thread weaving through time.
